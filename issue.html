Bridging the STEM Gap for Georgia's Youth

STEM Skills Are in High Demand[1]

STEM careers are growing much faster than most fields. By 2033, the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics projects:

10% GROWTH

Projected increase in STEM jobs

$100K MEDIAN SALARY

Median STEM wages, well above the national average

In Georgia, some STEM careers are considered “hot jobs” because they offer strong growth, high salaries, and plenty of opportunities. Explore a few examples below:[2]

Mechanical Engineer Design and develop mechanical systems for a range of industries.
“Hot Job” = Above-average growth, above-average pay, 100+ annual openings in Georgia
Software Developer Create applications and systems for computers and mobile devices.
“Hot Job” = Above-average growth, above-average pay, 100+ annual openings in Georgia
Web Developer Build and maintain websites and web applications.
“Hot Job” = Above-average growth, above-average pay, 100+ annual openings in Georgia
Electronics Eng. Technician Test, repair, and maintain electronic equipment and systems.
“Hot Job” = Above-average growth, above-average pay, 100+ annual openings in Georgia

These careers are just a few examples of the many opportunities available in Georgia’s growing STEM economy.

Too Many Georgia Students Are Still Falling Behind[3]

of 5th graders reached science proficiency on the Georgia Milestones assessment

Learning Happens Beyond the Classroom[4]

Students spend the majority of their waking hours outside of school. Afterschool time is a powerful window for hands-on, curiosity-driven learning.

80%

of student's waking hours are spent outside of school

Out of School
In School

High-quality afterschool STEM programs have been proven to boost: [5]

STEM Interest and Identity

Fostering a passion for science, technology, engineering, and math, and building a sense of belonging in STEM fields.

Career Awareness and Readiness

Developing awareness of STEM careers and building skills necessary for future academic and professional success.

Problem-Solving and Perseverance

Encouraging creative problem-solving, resilience, and persistence in the face of challenges.

For example:

  • Chicago’s Science Club saw STEM college majors jump from 1% to 34% [6]
  • The Clubhouse Network helped 80% of alumni enter STEM careers [7]

We’re Working Towards Closing the Gap[8]

Wealthier families invest over 3.5x more in out-of-school enrichment than lower-income families. This is how STEM access gaps widen.
